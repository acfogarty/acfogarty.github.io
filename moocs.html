MOOC reviews

Deep Learning Specialisation

Andrew Ng, deeplearning.ai (Coursera)
  • Content: Five courses, starting from the basics of neural nets, and finishing with CNNs and RNNs. Some deep learning subjects not treated, e.g. variational autoencoders
  • Review: Great course, same level of quality as Andrew Ng's well-known Machine Learning course. The exercises are very easy, just fill-in-the-blanks in jupyter notebooks (lots of hand-holding, not too much independent thinking needed)
  • Easiness: 4 / 5
  • Clarity: 5 / 5
  • I completed: all videos and notebooks for all courses, paid validated certificate for one course

Natural Language Processing

Anna Potapenko, National Research University - Higher School of Economics (Coursera)
  • Content: Text classification, language modeling, word embeddings, encoder-decoder architecture for translation
  • Review: Part of the Deep Learning specialisation by the Russian HSE university. Great instructor, high quality slides and exercises. Exercises are fill-in-the-blanks jupyter notebooks, but require more effort and autonomous thinking than Andrew Ng's deep learning specialisation.
  • Easiness: 3 / 5
  • Clarity: 5 / 5
  • I completed: all videos, quizzes and exercises, validated certificate

Divide and Conquer, Sorting and Searching, and Randomized Algorithms

Tim Roughgarden, Stanford University (Coursera)
  • Content: How to find Big O complexity for a selection of famous algorithms (Mergesort, Quicksort, Contraction Algorithm for finding the min cut in a graph)
  • Review: Clear explanations. Detailed demonstration for every algo. Aimed at people who want to determine Big O themselves, not just people who want a basic understanding of Big O.
  • Easiness: 3 / 5
  • Clarity: 4 / 5
  • I completed: all videos, some exos

Mathematics for Machine Learning: PCA

Marc P. Deisenroth, Imperial College London (Coursera)
  • Content: Details of the mathematics behind Principal Component Analysis
  • Review: There's a huge difference in difficulty between the first week (how to find the mean of a dataset) and the fourth week (a derivation of PCA more detailed than 90% of the method's users have probably ever seen).
  • Easiness: 2 / 5
  • Clarity: 4 / 5
  • I completed: all videos, some exos

Practical Time Series Analysis

William Thistleton, Tural Sadigov, The State University of New York (Coursera)
  • Content: All the ingredients of SARIMA, MA, AR, ARMA, ARIMA, SARIMAX
  • Review: The course is practical and not theoretical. The programming language is R.
  • Easiness: 2 / 5
  • Clarity: 4 / 5
  • I completed: all videos, some exos

Fundamentals of Graphic Design

California Institute of the Arts (Coursera)
  • Content: How to choose a typeface, how to tell which colours go together, fundamentials of composition and contrast
  • Review: Extremely basic. Useful for people like me who need to design their own UI for webapps, and have no clue about graphic design, but just want to make their interface a bit less ugly
  • Easiness: 5 / 5
  • Clarity: 5 / 5
  • I completed: watched videos only

Information Theory

The Chinese University of Hong Kong (Coursera)
  • Review: This is not a true online course, it's more like an audiobook of a pretty dry textbook. In other words, the lecturer simply reads aloud his (text-heavy, image-light) slides. Did not finish.
  • Easiness: N/A
  • Clarity: N/A
  • I completed: abandoned after first week

Discrete Optimization

Pascal Van Hentenryck, The University of Melbourne (Coursera)
  • Content: Comprehensive overview of discrete optimization methods: constraint programming, dynamic programming, local search, linear programming...
  • Review: Fun instructor, full of energy, and fun examples that liven up the course. The exercises demand a lot of time and effort. They are wide open, no handholding.
  • Easiness: 3 / 5
  • Clarity: 4 / 5
  • I completed: in progress

Advanced Styling with Responsive Design

Colleen van Lent, University of Michigan (Coursera)
  • Content: Responsive website design, including bootstrap
  • Review: Great lecturer, but the pace was very slow and simple. Aimed at people with little programming experience. You can probably learn the same info in an hour by reading a few articles.
  • Easiness: 5 / 5
  • Clarity: 5 / 5
  • I completed: watched videos only

Mathematics for Machine Learning: Linear Algebra

Imperial College London (Coursera)
  • Content: Exactly what it says in the title
  • Review: The first week is high-school level, but the later weeks are much more advanced. Useful if you've forgotten or never encountered the Gram-Schmidt process, Einstein summation convention, etc. How to do matrix inversion, eigenbasis etc. with pencil and paper. More detailed than needed for practical applications e.g. if you know what an inverse matrix is and how to obtain one using your favourite package, then maybe you don't care if you've forgotten how to do the inversion by hand.
  • Easiness: 2 / 5
  • Clarity: 4 / 5
  • I completed: videos and quizzes

Bayesian Methods for Machine Learning

Daniil Polykovskiy, Alexander Novikov, National Research University - Higher School of Economics (Coursera)
  • Content: E-M algorithm, variational inference, Latent Dirichlet Allocation, Markov chain Monte Carlo
  • Review: Part of the Deep Learning specialisation by the Russian HSE university. Like the other courses in the specialisation, the slides are high quality. Mathematical content is higher than in most deep learning MOOCs.
  • Easiness: 3 / 5
  • Clarity: 5 / 5
  • I completed: in progress

SQL for Data Science

Sadie St. Lawrence, University of California, Davis (Coursera)
  • Content: Basic introduction to SQL
  • Review: This course is for absolute beginners, aimed at people who don't know any SQL, have little or no programming experience, and have never taken an online course of any kind.
  • Easiness: 5 / 5
  • Clarity: 5 / 5
  • I completed: did not finish; watched some videos only
